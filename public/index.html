OUR STORY

I built MemoriGems for the people I love — and the moments I didn't want to lose.

MemoriGems began the way most meaningful things do: in a quiet moment of grief. Watching someone I love begin to forget — and realizing how much of who they are lives in their voice, their stories, their way of saying my name.

I searched for something that could hold all of it: the photos, the recordings, the songs that made them smile when nothing else did. I couldn't find anything that felt like it was made by someone who'd actually sat in those rooms.

So I started building it. For families like mine. For caregivers carrying more than anyone sees. For the grandchildren who deserve to know who their grandmother really was.

I wasn't alone in this. My co-founder, Edwina Castone, has walked this road with me since before MemoriGems had a name. Her belief, her care, and her work shaped this from the very first conversation. There would be no MemoriGems without her.

— Lisette Diaz Santiago
