WHAT IS A GEM

A gem is a moment of connection, kept.

A gem is the smallest, most human unit of connection — a voice note, a story, a photograph, a song. Each one is tied to a person, a place, and a feeling, so it can be found again the way the heart finds things: not by searching, but by recognizing.

A voice saying your name. A story only they could tell. A song that still reaches them.
RECOGNITION MATTERS

Photos ask us to remember.
Voice helps us feel recognized.

Most memory tools are built around recall — names, dates, the order things happened. But connection doesn't live in recall. It lives in recognition: the warmth that arrives before a name does, the ease in the body when a familiar voice speaks.

When memory changes, recall may be the first thing to go. Recognition often stays — and so does the relationship it carries. MemoriGems is built for what stays.

Recognition is often just as important as recall.

WHY VOICE MATTERS

Voice carries what other media leave behind.

A photograph shows a face. A voice carries a whole person — identity, emotion, and the unmistakable feeling of being known. That's why everything in MemoriGems begins with voice.

Identity
A voice is as singular as a fingerprint — the sound of one specific person, alive in the room.
Emotion
Tone reaches us before words do. A familiar voice can comfort when nothing else can.
Belonging
The way someone says your name tells you that you belong to them — and they to you.
Continuity
A voice can be returned to, again and again, long after the moment itself has passed.

A REAL FAMILY STORY

The grandfather who knew her voice — long after her name.

Before MemoriGems was a company, it was a granddaughter sitting beside her grandfather as dementia slowly took the names, the dates, the most recent days.

What it couldn't take was recognition. He knew the sound of her voice. He softened at a familiar song. He returned, for a moment, when the right story was told.

That was the discovery at the heart of MemoriGems: when memory changes, connection can still find a way through. We built the platform we wished we'd had in that room.

— Lisette's story, and the reason MemoriGems exists

WHY WE BUILT MEMORIGEMS

I built MemoriGems for the people I love — and the moments I didn't want to lose.

MemoriGems began the way most meaningful things do: in a quiet moment of grief. Watching someone I love begin to forget — and realizing how much of who they are lives in their voice, their stories, their way of saying my name.

I searched for something that could hold all of it: the photos, the recordings, the songs that made them smile when nothing else did. I couldn't find anything that felt like it was made by someone who'd actually sat in those rooms.

So I started building it. For families like mine. For caregivers carrying more than anyone sees. For the grandchildren who deserve to know who their grandmother really was.

I wasn't alone in this. My co-founder, Edwina Castone, has walked this road with me since before MemoriGems had a name. Her belief, her care, and her work shaped this from the very first conversation. There would be no MemoriGems without her.

— Lisette Diaz Santiago

THE FUTURE WE'RE BUILDING

We're not building a memory product.
We're building a connection company.

Dementia and memory care are where MemoriGems begins — because that's where the need is most urgent and most tender. But they're not the limit of the vision.
