WHY WE CREATED LITTLEGEMS

We built MemoriGems to preserve connection.
We built LittleGems to begin it.

MemoriGems was inspired by families navigating memory change — by the quiet discovery that connection can outlast memory itself.

But that journey taught us something we didn't expect. Connection doesn't begin when memories fade. It begins in childhood — in the very first stories a child tells, and the very first people they love out loud.

So we made LittleGems: a place for children to build the habits that make connection last. Reflection. Empathy. Gratitude. Storytelling. Emotional awareness. The simple, repeatable practice of noticing the people who matter.

Connection deserves preservation — and it begins long before memory matters.

THE THINKING BEHIND THE PLAY

Small things, doing quiet, important work.

Children's drawings are early stories.

Before a child can write their life, they draw it. Every picture is a first attempt at "this is what mattered to me."

Relationships are built in small moments.

Connection isn't taught in a single lesson. It's built in little moments, noticed and repeated, until they become a bond.

Reflection builds emotional awareness.

Naming a feeling is the first step to understanding it — in themselves, and then in the people around them.

Gratitude strengthens connection.

Noticing what we're thankful for is how children learn to see — and truly value — the people in their lives.

Storytelling strengthens belonging.

A child who can tell their own story understands where they belong in it, and who they belong to.

Small moments become identity.

The moments a child chooses to keep become part of how they understand who they are.

THREE LITTLE GAMES

Gentle games that practice connection.

EMOTIONAL LITERACY
Match the feeling to the moment
Pair each feeling with the moment that gave it — a gentle way to put words to big feelings, one gem at a time.
EMPATHY & KINDNESS
Choose the kindest thing to do
Read a little moment, then tap the kindest choice — a gentle way to practice empathy and good decisions.
CALM & SELF-REGULATION
Breathe a gem calm
Pick a gem, then breathe along slowly as it grows and shrinks — a gentle way to settle a racing heart.
WAYS TO USE THEM

Small moments, tenderly held.

Give to a grandparent

A finished gem becomes a small letter from a child — a moment they made just for someone they love.

Tuck into a memory box

Keep a child's gems somewhere safe. Years from now, those drawings will sound exactly like who they were.

Share them as conversation

Sit with a child and a gem. Ask the questions out loud. Let them tell you about the people and moments they love.
